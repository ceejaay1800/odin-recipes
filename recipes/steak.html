Pan Seared Steak

Description:

This 20-minute recipe is done on the stovetop in one pan (no need to finish it in the oven). This is one of my favorite steak recipes and I make it year-round because it’s such a quick and convenient cooking method. That garlic butter is lip-smacking good! Read on for great tips on how to improve beef sustainability, reduce food waste and you will love my ideas for easy meal prep with leftover steak.

Ingredients:

  • New York Steak
  • Salt
  • Black Pepper
  • Butter
  • Garlic
  • Rosemary

Steps:

  • Step 1: Pat dry, use paper towels to pat the steaks dry to get a perfect sear and reduce oil splatter.
  • Step 2: Season generously,just before cooking steaks, sprinkle both sides liberally with salt and pepper.
  • Step 3: Preheat the pan on medium and brush with oil. Using just 1/2 Tbsp oil reduces splatter.
  • Step 4: Sear steaks – add steaks and sear each side 3-4 minutes until a brown crust has formed then use tongs to turn steaks on their sides and sear edges (1 min per edge).
  • Step 5: Add butter and aromatics – melt in butter with quartered garlic cloves and rosemary sprigs. Tilt pan to spoon garlic butter over steaks and cook to your desired doneness,
  • Step 6: Remove steak and rest 10 minutes before slicing against the grain.
